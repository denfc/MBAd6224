The Ancient Concept Known as a Book

No book is assigned for this course. In this open-source, internet era, the transmission of information occurs in many different ways. As much as I like books, I see no need to ask you to buy one when the material for which you may need explanation(s) is available for free, or in the case of the second book referenced below, for $2.99. Those in the Health Care portion of the course should find that book especially worthwhile because of the author's background and his many examples from clinical trials.

As a way to help us all, students and instructors, we should set up a reference page in Blackboard, across the entire course, where people can share resources they found helpful. (15 July 2016)

  1. Book 1: Business Analytics: Data Analysis & Decision Making, 6th Edition
  2. Book 2: The Little Handbook of Statistical Practice
  3. Book 3: Online Statistics Education: An Interactive Multimedia Course of Study
